Personal Action Plan

Assignment 5

Identifying issues
  • Stay up to date with technology, especially newer industries with less regulation. I will also do my best to stay up to date with any new legislation, loopholes, and consider different perspectives on different topics that may not align with my own opinions.
  • Discuss topics with coworkers to inform myself or learn about a different opinion. This will help deepen my knowledge and help build awareness of how others perceive different issues/topics.
  • Cross check sources and information in articles about different topics to ensure I am getting facts and unbiased information.
  • Spend about 10-15 minutes a day in the morning reading technology news (Flipboard, TechCrunch, Wired, Social media, etc.)
In my personal life
  • Keep a list of companies that have ethical issues and try to avoid using services or products from them. On the flip side, keep a list of companies with good track records and consider that when choosing between alternatives.
  • Talk to friends and family who may be less informed about technologies/companies that are unethical and may lead to a cyber dystopia.
  • Try to avoid becoming reliant on technology. Do as much as I can offline without too much of an impact on my productivity.
  • Take control of my personal data. Take the time to go into settings and limit the amount of information that services can access, track, and sell.
In the workplace
  • Put myself in the shoes of the consumers. Ask myself if I would be comfortable using the product I am building, and also if my product is taking advantage of the average consumer. Take the extra time to consider this instead of blindly following orders.
  • Speak up if I feel something is unethical. Even if it is a manager. It is better to raise the issue and at least get people talking than to not raise the issue at all and push something unethical out to consumers.
  • Build loyalty to values rather than a company. By building my morals around certain values I do not become biased to a company and am more likely to notice unethical decisions and also take action against them.
  • Pursue employment elsewhere if I feel that I am supporting an unethical company despite my best efforts to make change. If all my efforts lead to little change, just leave so that I can make a positive impact somewhere else.
In society and policy
  • Whenever I find out about an ethical issue in the news, try to see if there are any causes fighting against it. Then spread word against the company/industry through social media, word of mouth, and also by signing petitions or showing concern to local representatives.

  • Consider other audiences outside of myself when thinking about ethical issues. Just because something may benefit or not affect me does not mean that something is ethical.
